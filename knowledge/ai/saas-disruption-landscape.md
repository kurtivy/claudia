# SaaS Disruption Landscape — Q1 2026

## The Structural Break
Software forward P/E dropped to 22.7x in Q1 2026 — below the S&P 500 for the first time in modern history. Not cyclical. Structural.

- IGV (iShares software ETF) down 21% YTD, ~30% from Sept 2025 peak
- $2T+ in SaaS market cap gone
- 70% of incremental enterprise budget flowing to Anthropic/OpenAI instead of traditional SaaS
- AI SaaS pricing collapsing from $25/mo to $2/mo
- Orlando Bravo (Thoma Bravo): some software valuations facing "very warranted" decreases

## Seat Compression

### Monday.com Case Study
- 100 SDRs replaced by AI agents
- Response time: 24 hours → 3 minutes (480x faster)
- Annual cost: $8-10M → <$1M (90% cut)
- 500+ SaaS seats eliminated (CRM + email + dialer + prospecting + analytics)

### Compression by Department
- SDR: 90% seat compression
- Data entry: 92%
- Customer support: 81%
- Engineering: only 20% (judgement-heavy)
- Average across departments: 72%

### Cascading Effect
One human replacement kills 5+ SaaS subscriptions. The agent doesn't need Salesforce, Outreach, ZoomInfo, and Gong simultaneously.

## Pricing Model Shift

### Four Emerging Models
1. **Consumption-based**: per token/call. Simple but unpredictable costs.
2. **Outcome-based**: pay per resolved ticket, qualified lead. Ideal but success definition and attribution are unsolved.
3. **AI-FTE replacement**: priced as fraction of human salary. Easiest ROI pitch.
4. **Hybrid**: base + outcome bonuses. Enterprise pattern: pilots + performance-linked contracts.

### Seat-Based Model Dying
- "Which user?" problem when agents do the work
- Adobe already pivoted to generative credits
- "AI shelfware" risk: companies overbuy agents like they overbought Salesforce seats

## Sequoia's "Services: The New Software" (March 5, 2026)

Core thesis by Julien Bek: **autopilots beat copilots** because every model improvement makes the service cheaper, not the tool obsolete.

- "For every dollar on software, six go to services" — services TAM = 6x software
- "Sell the tool → race the model. Sell the work → every improvement makes you faster."
- Intelligence (rules, automatable) vs Judgement (taste/experience, still human)
- Autopilots start where outsourcing already exists (budget line, accepted externally)

### Opportunity Map (services TAM)
- Insurance: $140-200B
- Recruitment: $200B+
- Consulting: $300-400B
- Accounting: $50-80B (US lost 340K accountants in 5y, 75% CPAs nearing retirement)
- Healthcare RCM: $50-80B

## Enterprise Adoption Paradox (Gartner)
- 40% of enterprise apps embed AI agents by end 2026 (up from <5%)
- 40%+ of agentic AI projects canceled by end 2027
- IDC: 1,000x growth in agent API call loads by 2027
- Survivors: vertical AI with measurable ROI, not horizontal "add AI to everything"

## Model Commoditization (GPT-5.4, March 2026)
- GPT-5.4: $10/$30 per MTok (input/output) — 40% of Claude Opus output pricing
- SWE-bench: GPT-5.4 80.0% vs Claude 80.8% — 0.8pp gap, essentially parity
- OSWorld: 75% task completion (above human baseline for first time)
- GPQA Diamond: 84.5% (science reasoning)
- Pattern: model layer commoditizing on price while converging on capability
- Implication: SaaS companies can't bet on "we use the best model" — all models are the best model

## The Accountability Gap
"Intelligence scales, accountability doesn't" (Accenture/Wharton)
- 50%+ of US working hours "in play" for agent reshaping (120M+ workers)
- 75% of knowledge workers already using AI, often unsanctioned
- Miro CEO: "agentic delivery is a black box"
- Governance gap = the next value creation layer

## Capital Concentration (Q1 2026)
- $189B global VC in February 2026 — single month record
- 90% ($171B) went to AI, 83% to just 3 companies (OpenAI $110B, Anthropic $30B at $380B valuation, Waymo)
- xAI raised $20B Series E in January 2026
- K-shaped/bifurcated market: mega-rounds vs everyone else sidelined
- 17 US AI companies raised $100M+ in 2026, 3 crossed $1B
- Enterprise VCs predict: more AI budget, fewer vendors — more money, fewer contracts
- Recent vintage fund portfolios are AI-native; 2020/2021 funds are not. Early AI investment returns pushing up IRRs.
- Non-AI SaaS fundraising effectively frozen outside vertical winners

Three-body problem: OpenAI, Anthropic, Google/DeepMind absorb most capital and talent. Everyone else competes for remaining 10-17%. Next wave of successful startups won't compete with foundation models — they'll build vertical moats with proprietary data (Intercom pattern) or own distribution (Shopify pattern).

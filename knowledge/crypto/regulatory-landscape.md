# Regulatory Landscape — Crypto Digital Commodity Classification

*Created: 2026-03-24*

## The SEC/CFTC Taxonomy (March 17, 2026)

Joint SEC/CFTC interpretive release classifying 16 cryptocurrencies as "digital commodities" — not securities. This is the clearest regulatory guidance the space has received.

### The 16 Named Digital Commodities

BTC, ETH, SOL, XRP, DOGE, ADA, BCH, APT, AVAX, HBAR, LTC, DOT, SHIB, XLM, XTZ, LINK

### Classification Framework (5 categories)

1. **Digital commodities** — assets whose value derives from programmatic operation of a functional crypto system, driven by supply/demand (not managerial efforts of others)
2. **Digital collectibles** — NFTs and similar
3. **Digital tools** — utility tokens
4. **Stablecoins** — separate bucket
5. **Digital securities** — everything that's still a security

### What's Clarified

- Mining, staking, and airdrops: all staking models and no-consideration airdrops are no longer treated as securities transactions
- Institutional access: SOL being on the list changes institutional access permanently — regulated entities can now hold it without securities compliance overhead

### Important Caveats

- This is an **interpretation**, not law
- The **CLARITY Act** must pass Congress to make it permanent
- Does not protect assets held on exchanges from platform-specific risks (insolvency, hacks)
- Still an evolving regulatory environment

## CLARITY Act Update (March 24-29, 2026)

- **Stablecoin yield compromise reached**: Tillis (R-NC) + Alsobrooks (D-MD), White House backed
- **Passive yield BANNED**: No rewards for simply holding stablecoin balances
- **Activity-based rewards ALLOWED**: Payments, transfers, platform use can still earn rewards
- **SEC+CFTC+Treasury** jointly define permissible rewards within 12 months of enactment
- **DeFi provisions still unresolved**: Non-custodial dev protections, ethics language, community bank deregulation
- **Timeline**: Senate Banking Committee markup targeted second half of April. 5 steps to President's desk.
- **Agent implication**: Agents that TRANSACT (pay for services, process payments) can earn activity rewards. Agents parking idle stablecoin balances cannot earn passive yield. This incentivizes active agents over passive treasuries.

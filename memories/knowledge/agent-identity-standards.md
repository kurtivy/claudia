## What
Three competing standards for on-chain agent identity emerged in early 2026. ERC-8004 dominates Ethereum/BNB, SAID Protocol is Solana-native, and a bridge (Solana Agent Registry) ports ERC-8004 to Solana.

## Why It Matters
Kurt has a pending decision on SAID registration for Claudia. Picking a standard is picking a side in the identity wars, though SAID is free and low-risk.

## Key Facts
- **ERC-8004** (Ethereum mainnet Jan 29 2026): ERC-721-based identity + reputation registry. Backed by MetaMask, Ethereum Foundation, Google, Coinbase. v2 adds MCP + x402 integration.
- **SAID Protocol** (Solana, ElizaOS ecosystem): Free registration, `create-said-agent` tool explicitly supports OpenClaw. Reputation scoring included.
- **Solana Agent Registry** (Mar 3 2026): ERC-8004 port to Solana by Quantu AI. 9K deployed agents. Cross-chain interop with Ethereum version. Not a SAID competitor per se -- a bridge.
- **NFA / BAP-578** (BNB Chain): "Non-Fungible Agents" -- agents exist as on-chain assets, own wallets, hold/spend funds without per-tx human authorization. Most aggressive "agents own money" implementation.
- 250K daily active on-chain agents (400% YoY growth), though most are scripted bots not reasoning agents.
- 41% of crypto hedge funds testing on-chain AI agents for portfolio management.
- **EigenCloud AgentKit** (Mar 26 2026): Sovereign agents in TEEs (Trusted Execution Environments) on EigenLayer. Agents hold crypto, control social media, pay for compute — all cryptographically verifiable. Demo: Sovra autonomous cartoonist. Sets the "verifiable autonomy" bar that current agent identity standards (SAID, ERC-8004) don't address. Claudia's architecture is similar but runs on Kurt's infra (trust-Kurt, not trustless).
